Arlington's Central DFW Air Quality Profile

Arlington sits in the heart of the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, experiencing the combined air quality challenges of both cities. With a population exceeding 400,000, Arlington is one of the largest cities in Texas without a public transit system, resulting in high vehicle traffic and elevated particulate matter levels that find their way into residential HVAC systems.

The city's housing stock spans from 1950s-era developments near UTA to modern master-planned communities like Viridian. Regardless of your home's age, Arlington's extreme summer heat (averaging 96–101°F) forces HVAC systems into extended operation, circulating accumulated dust, allergens, and microbial growth throughout your living spaces.

Arlington's proximity to both Dallas and Fort Worth means residents face pollen from diverse vegetation zones — urban tree canopies, prairie grasslands, and post oak forests all contribute to elevated allergen levels that demand professional allergen reduction through regular air duct cleaning.

Arlington Climate & IAQ Factors

  • Central DFW heat island: Urban development intensifies summer temperatures, increasing HVAC demands
  • Traffic particulate: I-20/I-30/Hwy 360 corridor generates significant vehicle exhaust and particulate matter
  • Diverse pollen sources: Mixed vegetation from the Cross Timbers ecoregion produces year-round allergen exposure
  • Humidity: 55–70% average relative humidity creates conditions for mold and microbial growth in ductwork
  • Varied housing stock: 1950s to modern construction means diverse ductwork cleaning needs
