Austin's Allergy Capital: Why Duct Cleaning is Essential

Austin consistently ranks among the worst cities in America for allergy sufferers. The infamous "Cedar Fever" season (December through February) produces extreme Ashe juniper pollen counts that blanket the Hill Country. But the allergy challenges don't stop there — oak pollen peaks in March–April, followed by grass pollen through summer, and ragweed in the fall.

These allergens infiltrate your home through windows, doors, and your HVAC system. Once inside your ductwork, they become trapped and continuously recirculate every time your system runs. For Austin residents, this creates a year-round cycle of indoor air quality (IAQ) problems that can trigger asthma, allergic rhinitis, and other respiratory conditions.

Professional air duct cleaning with HEPA filtration is the most effective method of allergen reduction for Austin homes. Our NADCA-certified technicians remove accumulated pollen, dust, and particulate matter from your entire duct system, providing immediate relief during peak allergy seasons.

Austin Climate & IAQ Factors

  • Cedar fever: Ashe juniper pollen counts reach extreme levels (>5,000 grains/m³) from December–February
  • Year-round allergens: Oak (March–April), grass (May–September), ragweed (August–November) create continuous pollen exposure
  • Summer heat: 100°F+ temperatures from June through September keep HVAC systems running 12+ hours daily
  • Humidity variability: Austin swings between humid and dry conditions, creating varying mold and microbial growth risks
  • Rapid growth: Construction dust from Austin's development boom enters homes through HVAC systems
