Houston's Unique Indoor Air Quality Challenges

Houston presents the most demanding indoor air quality environment in Texas. With average relative humidity of 75–90% year-round, the Gulf Coast climate creates ideal conditions for microbial growth inside HVAC ductwork. Mold, mildew, bacteria, and fungi thrive in the dark, moist environment of your air conditioning system — especially on evaporator coils and inside supply ducts.

Houston's HVAC systems run nearly year-round, with summer cooling seasons extending from April through November. This means your ductwork is constantly moving air, and any accumulated particulate matter, mold spores, or allergens are continuously circulated throughout your living spaces.

Post-hurricane and flooding events — a recurring reality for Houston homeowners — can introduce additional moisture and contaminants into HVAC systems. Even homes that didn't flood may experience elevated humidity levels that penetrate ductwork, making professional air duct cleaning and UV light installation essential for Houston residences.

Houston Climate & IAQ Factors

  • Extreme humidity: 75–90% average relative humidity — the highest of any major Texas city — creates constant mold risk
  • Extended cooling season: 8+ months of AC usage circulates more contaminants than virtually any other U.S. city
  • Subtropical pollen: Oak, pine, ragweed, and grass pollen seasons overlap, maintaining elevated allergen levels year-round
  • Flood risk: Recurring hurricanes and tropical storms introduce moisture contamination to HVAC systems
  • Urban particulate: Houston's petrochemical corridor and highway traffic contribute elevated outdoor PM2.5 levels that enter homes
