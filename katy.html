Katy's Growing Community and IAQ Concerns

Katy has been one of the fastest-growing communities in Texas for over a decade, and with good reason — top-rated schools, master-planned communities, and family-friendly amenities make it one of West Houston's most desirable areas. However, Katy's location in the Katy Prairie and along the Buffalo Bayou presents significant indoor air quality (IAQ) challenges.

The flat prairie terrain and proximity to the Gulf of Mexico result in 75–85% average relative humidity throughout the summer months. This moisture-rich environment creates persistent conditions for microbial growth inside HVAC ductwork, particularly on evaporator coils and in supply ducts where condensation occurs.

Additionally, Katy was one of the hardest-hit areas during Hurricane Harvey and has experienced subsequent flooding events. Many homes that were affected — even those that weren't flooded — experienced elevated indoor humidity that can introduce mold spores and contaminants into HVAC systems. Regular professional air duct cleaning with HEPA filtration is essential for maintaining healthy indoor air in Katy.

Katy Climate & IAQ Factors

  • Prairie humidity: 75–85% summer humidity from Gulf moisture and flat, water-retaining terrain
  • Flood-prone geography: Buffalo Bayou watershed and Katy Prairie create recurring flood risk and moisture exposure
  • Rapid development: Construction dust from extensive new home building enters HVAC systems
  • Extended HVAC season: 9+ months of air conditioning usage in subtropical West Houston climate
  • Prairie grasses: Agricultural pollen and grass allergens from the surrounding Katy Prairie
