San Antonio's Climate and Indoor Air Quality

San Antonio sits at the intersection of South Central Texas's semi-arid and subtropical climate zones, creating a unique set of indoor air quality (IAQ) challenges. Summer temperatures frequently exceed 100°F, and humidity levels can spike to 70–80% during Gulf moisture surges, creating conditions ripe for microbial growth inside HVAC ductwork.

San Antonio's position along the Balcones Escarpment also places it squarely in the path of Central Texas's notorious mountain cedar (Ashe juniper) pollen, which produces debilitating allergies from December through February. Combined with year-round grass and ragweed pollen, San Antonio homeowners face continuous particulate matter infiltration into their HVAC systems.

Many San Antonio homes — particularly in established neighborhoods like Alamo Heights, Monte Vista, and the King William District — feature older HVAC systems with ductwork that may not have been cleaned in decades. These older systems are especially vulnerable to accumulated contaminants and benefit significantly from professional air duct cleaning.

San Antonio Climate & IAQ Factors

  • Extreme heat: 100°F+ summer temperatures from June–September force extended HVAC operation
  • Humidity surges: Gulf moisture pushes humidity to 70–80% seasonally, fostering microbial growth in ductwork
  • Cedar and pollen: Balcones Escarpment location means heavy cedar, oak, grass, and ragweed pollen exposure
  • Older housing stock: Many neighborhoods feature homes 30–60+ years old with aging ductwork systems
  • Military relocations: Frequent move-ins require duct inspection and cleaning for incoming families
